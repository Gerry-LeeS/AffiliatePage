Responsible Gambling

Play Safe, Play Smart

Gambling should be an enjoyable form of entertainment, not a way to make money or escape problems. We are committed to promoting responsible gambling and encouraging our users to play safely, within their limits.

Play for Entertainment, Not Income

Casino games are designed to be fun, but they always carry a risk of losing money. There is no guaranteed way to win, and losses are a normal part of gambling.

Golden Rule

Only gamble with money you can afford to lose, and never chase losses.

Set Limits

Responsible gambling starts with setting clear personal limits:

  • Time limits – Decide how long you will play before you start.
  • Budget limits – Set a spending amount and stick to it.
  • Loss limits – Accept losses as part of the experience and avoid trying to recover them.

Many online casinos offer tools that allow players to set deposit limits, wagering limits, or time-out periods. We recommend using these features to maintain control.

Know the Signs of Problem Gambling

Gambling may become a problem if you:

  • Spend more time or money than planned
  • Feel stressed, anxious, or guilty about gambling
  • Gamble to escape personal or financial problems
  • Chase losses or borrow money to gamble
  • Neglect work, family, or social responsibilities

If gambling stops being fun, it's time to take a break.

Take Breaks or Self-Exclude

If you feel your gambling is getting out of control, consider:

  • Taking a temporary break from gambling
  • Using self-exclusion tools provided by casinos
  • Closing gambling accounts entirely if needed

Self-exclusion allows you to restrict access to gambling services for a set period of time. This can be a crucial step in regaining control.

UK Self-Exclusion Scheme

GAMSTOP is the UK's national self-exclusion scheme. Register at www.gamstop.co.uk to exclude yourself from all UK-licensed gambling sites for a period of 6 months, 1 year, or 5 years.

Protect Minors

Gambling is strictly for adults only. We strongly encourage parents and guardians to:

  • Use parental control software on devices
  • Keep gambling account details private and secure
  • Monitor online activity on shared devices
  • Educate children about the risks of gambling

18+ Only: It is illegal for anyone under 18 to gamble in the UK. All operators we recommend have strict age verification measures in place.

Get Help and Support

If you or someone you know may have a gambling problem, confidential help is available. You don't have to face this alone.

Support Organizations

  • BeGambleAware – www.begambleaware.org
    Free helpline: 0808 8020 133
  • GamCare – www.gamcare.org.uk
    Live chat and phone support available
  • Gamblers Anonymous UK – www.gamblersanonymous.org.uk
    Free peer support meetings nationwide
  • National Gambling Helpline
    Phone: 0808 8020 133 (24/7 freephone)

Professional support can help you regain control and make positive changes. Seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness.
